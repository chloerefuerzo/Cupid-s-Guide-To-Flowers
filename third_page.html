CUPID'S GUIDE TO FLOWERS

Common flowers to give someone you cherish


Rose

Roses were associated with Aphrodite (Venus), the goddess of love. According to legend,
when her lover Adonis was wounded, her tears mixed with his blood and gave birth to the red
rose. In medieval times, the rose was linked to the Virgin Mary, symbolizing love and purity.
Poets like Shakespeare and writers throughout history have used roses as
metaphors for love, beauty, and passion. For example, in Romeo and Juliet,
Shakespeare wrote: “A rose by any other name would smell as sweet.”


Tulips

Tulips have roots in Persian and Ottoman cultures, where they were symbols of perfect
love. Tulips have a graceful, heart-shaped bloom when fully opened,
which subtly represents love and passion. Their elegant, soft
petals and bright colors make them an ideal flower for expressing romance.


Orchids

The word "orchid" comes from the Greek word Orchis, and ancient Greeks
associated it with fertility, love, and virility. Orchids have a unique, delicate, and long-lasting beauty, symbolizing
refined love and passion. Their symmetrical petals and striking colors make them a flower of
luxury, romance, and rare affection—ideal for expressing deep emotions.

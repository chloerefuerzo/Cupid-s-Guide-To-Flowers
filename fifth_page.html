CUPID'S GUIDE TO FLOWERS

Flowers to not give someone you cherish


Yellow Carnation

In Victorian flower language (floriography), yellow carnations were used to say “I reject you”
or “I am disappointed in you.” If given in a romantic relationship, they can unintentionally suggest lack of
feelings or dissatisfaction. Unlike red or pink carnations (which symbolize love and admiration), yellow carnations are
linked to gloom, regret, and jealousy. They were historically sent to decline a romantic confession.


Petunia

In floriography (the language of flowers), petunias are linked to frustration,
resentment, and even hostility—definitely not emotions you’d want to express to someone special.
If given in the wrong context, they could be misinterpreted as a sign of conflict rather than affection.


Yellow Chrysanthemums

In floriography, yellow chrysanthemums represent one-sided love or heartbreak.
Giving them to a romantic partner could unintentionally suggest that feelings are not mutual or that there is
distance in the relationship. Unlike red or pink flowers, which symbolize love and affection, yellow
chrysanthemums could be seen as a farewell flower rather than a loving gesture.
